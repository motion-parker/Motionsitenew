Visual identity

Secondary background: off-black #0A0A0A or #111111 for sectional rhythm

Secondary text: muted gray #9CA3AF
Tertiary text: lower gray #6B7280

Apple discipline applied to the dark palette
This is the most important section. The page must feel premium and quiet, not aggressive or edgy. Concretely:

Whitespace is the design. Use 2x to 3x more space between elements than feels natural. Generous internal padding on cards, large vertical gaps between sections.
Sectional rhythm. Alternate #000000 and #0A0A0A section by section, the way Apple alternates white and off-white on product pages. Creates calm visual cadence.
Type as the hero. No background images, no gradient blobs, no particle effects, no busy graphics. The typography itself carries the page. Each section opens with a massive headline given room to land.
Mixed-weight hierarchy. Headlines tight and heavy (700 to 900). Subheads and body light to regular (300 to 400). Apple uses weight contrast, not color contrast, to create hierarchy. Do the same.
Soft elevation, not harsh borders. Cards float on subtle shadows or quiet inner glows. Avoid bright borders. If a border is necessary, white at 8 to 10% opacity.
Barely-rounded corners. 6px to 8px max on buttons and cards. Modern but refined.
Lime green is punctuation. Lime appears as the "M" mark, primary CTA fills, key word emphasis, numbered badges, hover states. It never blankets a section. It accents.
Micro-interactions only. Subtle scroll fade-ins (600ms ease-out), soft hover lifts on cards (translate -2px, soft shadow), gentle button glow on hover. No flashy motion. No marquees. No parallax overkill.
No grain. No noise textures. Apple is clean. Stay clean.

Typography

Modern sans-serif. Inter, Geist, or SF Pro family. SF Pro Display for headlines if licensed; Inter Display works.
H1 (hero): 96px to 128px desktop, letter-spacing -0.03em, weight 700 to 800
H2 (section openers): 56px to 72px desktop, weight 700
Subheads: 22px to 28px, weight 300 to 400, capped at a comfortable reading line-length (max-width ~600px)
Body: 17px to 18px, weight 400, line-height 1.6
All text left-aligned by default (Apple discipline). Only the final closing CTA section uses center alignment.

Voice and copy direction

Casual, direct, punchy, human
Not corporate. No buzzwords. No "synergies".
Confident but quiet. Apple does not yell.
Short sentences. Hard truths over hype.
A local service business owner should read it and feel immediately understood.
Never use em dashes anywhere in the copy. Use periods, commas, colons, or regular hyphens with spaces. Non-negotiable.


Technical requirements

Single HTML file with embedded <style> and <script> blocks
Tailwind CSS via CDN acceptable
Inter or SF Pro via Google Fonts
Fully responsive, mobile-first
Semantic HTML5 (header, nav, main, section, footer)
Smooth scroll behavior on anchor links
Scroll-triggered fade-ins via Intersection Observer, around 600ms ease-out, slow and subtle
No external dependencies beyond Tailwind and Google Fonts
Test breakpoints: 375px, 768px, 1280px, 1920px
Accessibility: WCAG AA contrast (white-on-black is strong), semantic headings, alt text on images, visible focus states on all interactive elements


What this should NOT be

Not multi-page. One scroll. One path. One CTA.
Not aggressive or scrappy. Refined. Premium. Quiet confidence.
Not generic dark-mode SaaS with gradient blobs, floating 3D shapes, glassmorphism, or particle backgrounds
Not over-animated. No flashy motion. No marquees. No auto-playing video backgrounds.
Not crowded. If a section feels full, add 50% more whitespace.
No light mode anywhere
No em dashes in any copy
